Personal Trading System — Methodology v2

Design blueprint · prepared for independent council review · India (NSE) first, multi-market by design

Status: DESIGN BLUEPRINT (not yet empirically validated). This document specifies the logic, learning system, risk framework, and operating model. Every numeric threshold here is a starting hypothesis — the actual values are produced and validated by the Learning System (§7) against 10 years of Kite historical data. This blueprint is what the review council should pressure-test before we build.

1 · Philosophy & Objective

This is a trend-aligned, volatility-contraction breakout system with volatility-normalized risk. Its lineage is established momentum/trend work: Weinstein stage analysis (trade only uptrends), Minervini-style volatility contraction (the "coil"), O'Neil/Wyckoff institutional-footprint candles, and Van Tharp position sizing. It is not a high-win-rate system; it is a positive-skew system — many small, fast losses and a few large winners.

The objective function (locked)

We optimize for Calmar — annualized return ÷ maximum drawdown — i.e. expectancy per unit of drawdown, computed after costs. Win rate is a diagnostic, never a target.

Expectancy = (WinRate × Avg Win R) − (LossRate × Avg Loss R)

Why not maximize win rate

Pushing win rate (scaling out early, taking 2R fast) shrinks average R and usually lowers total expectancy. Breakout edges live on the right tail: ~35–45% win rate, but average winners of 3–6R. Optimizing Calmar keeps the system from "learning" its way into a fragile high-win-rate configuration that blows up in a bad streak.

Capital-protection model — and its honest limit

Risk is controlled by two deterministic dials on every trade: a hard stop-loss and a fixed-fractional risk-per-trade (RPT). Position size is set so a stop-out costs a fixed, small fraction of capital.

Critical truth: stops bound intraday loss, NOT gap loss

In swing trading the dominant tail is the overnight / earnings gap that opens below your stop — the stop then fills at the worse gap price, not your level. "We cannot lose more than X%" is true intraday and false across gaps. We mitigate gap risk by: (1) sizing that assumes a plausible adverse gap; (2) a hard earnings blackout; (3) caps on overnight and sector/correlated exposure; (4) a portfolio-level daily-loss kill-switch; (5) avoiding illiquid names where gaps are largest. The backtest must fill stops at the worse of stop-or-next-open so Calmar reflects this honestly.

4 · Signal Definitions parameterized & learnable

Every definition is a set of numbers with bounds. These are the knobs the Learning System tunes (§7). Values below are starting hypotheses.

Stage / trend filter (Weinstein)

Trade only Stage 2 (price above a rising long MA, higher highs) or confirmed Stage-1 breakouts. Reject Stage 3 (topping) and Stage 4 (declining). Long MA ≈ 30-week / 150-day, slope and distance thresholds learnable. Fixes vs. legacy: mutually-exclusive stage bands (no overlap), and the "higher highs" + volume gate restored.

PPC — Positive Pivotal Candle (institutional accumulation footprint)

  • Range expansion: candle TRP ≥ k₁ × its own average TRP
  • Bullish close: close in the upper k₂ of the range
  • Participation: volume ≥ k₃ × 20-day average and high delivery % (real delivery, not intraday squaring — a far better institutional signal than raw volume)
  • Net green candle

NPC — Negative Pivotal Candle

Bearish mirror of PPC. Primary use: sector-weakness detection (avoid strong stocks in distributing sectors) and short candidates in the US phase — not forced into the long watchlist.

Contraction (the coil)

True volatility compression before expansion: ATR declining (regression slope, not a 2-point ratio), candle ranges progressively narrowing (strict — fixes the legacy inverted tolerance), price coiling near resistance. The trigger bar's high becomes the entry trigger.

New features the data now supports

Delivery % (institutional conviction), relative-strength rank (candidate vs index/sector — a well-documented momentum edge), and computed sector strength (PPC-vs-NPC breadth per sector). These are added as features whose marginal edge is measured in the ablation study (§7), not assumed.

5 · Entry, Stop, Exit & Sizing

Entry

Trigger = high of the trigger bar. Enter on a confirmed break (the methodology's "last 30 minutes" discipline reduces intraday-noise whipsaws). Entry split 50/50 is itself a hypothesis to test vs. a single confirmed entry. Hard earnings blackout before entry.

Stop

Initial stop = entry − (TRP value). Stop is volatility-normalized per stock, never widened, trailed up only (breakeven → 2R → low-of-day method) as the trade extends.

Sizing

Base: fixed-fractional RPT (default 0.5%, regime-scaled). Optional, to be validated: a fractional-Kelly cap overlay for near-optimal growth with an explicit drawdown ceiling.

6 · Risk & Capital Protection

  • Per-trade: RPT 0.2–1.0% (default 0.5%), regime-scaled.
  • Portfolio: max total open risk ≤ 10% of capital; sector/correlation concentration caps.
  • Drawdown governor: auto de-risk (fewer/smaller positions) as month drawdown rises; hard freeze on new entries past a threshold.
  • Gap defense: earnings blackout, overnight-exposure cap, liquidity floor (avoid names where gaps are largest), resting stop orders at the broker (GTT), not just software stops.
  • Ruin control: sizing keeps the probability of a terminal drawdown negligible across Monte-Carlo trade-order simulations.

7 · The Learning System the core of the rebuild

Two distinct loops. Keeping them separate is what makes this learning rather than curve-fitting.

Loop 1 — Parameter learning ("threshold → outcome"), offline & validated

Every threshold in §4–5 is a knob. For each candidate set the honest backtest (costs, slippage, worse-of-stop-or-gap fills) reports win rate, avg R, after-cost expectancy, and max drawdown. A Bayesian search (Optuna) maps the surface and maximizes Calmar. The discipline that makes it real: optimize on a training window, then validate on a later window it has never seen; keep only thresholds that generalize; walk forward across 10 years to confirm stability. Winners become dated, versioned configs, promoted to live only with human sign-off. Re-runs quarterly (not nightly) — at ~5–20 live trades/month you learn from a decade of history, not last week.

Loop 2 — Attribution learning ("what's working now"), continuous

Every signal and trade (paper + live) is tagged with its features — stage, base quality, sector strength, delivery %, contraction tightness, regime — and its realized R. Over time this builds the evidence table (e.g., "PPC + delivery% > 60 + strong sector + trending regime → +1.4R over 80 trades; same in a weak sector → −0.2R"). It (a) biases live ranking toward what's paying, and (b) acts as a drift detector — when live results fall outside the backtest's expected band, it flags edge decay and triggers re-optimization.

Statistical guards (anti-self-deception)

  • Walk-forward / out-of-sample mandatory; no in-sample promotion.
  • Minimum trade counts; significance & minimum-improvement gates before any change.
  • Deflated performance metrics (penalize for the number of configurations tried).
  • Parameter-stability checks (drifting "best" values ⇒ noise, not edge).
  • Honest baselines: must beat buy-and-hold NIFTY and a plain momentum-factor index.

8 · Regime Contextualization "how markets are doing today"

The system reads current conditions via a measured regime, not a vibe: index trend (price vs. rising 150-DMA), breadth (% of universe in Stage 2 / above key MAs), volatility (India VIX, ATR percentile), and momentum-factor leadership. The regime scales three dials — RPT %, max concurrent positions, and signal selectivity. In weak/bearish regimes the system de-risks automatically (smaller, fewer, more selective — or flat). The regime mapping is itself learnable and validated, not hand-tuned.

9 · Operating Model — two real-money strategies ₹25k + ₹25k

Strategy S — Systematic (Claude-operated)

₹25,000. Trades only validated signals, fully rules-based, all guardrails enforced in deterministic code. This is the machine.

Strategy D — Discretionary (you)

₹25,000. You take the calls; the system provides decision support (setup cards, sizing, risk, journal). This is the human.

Run in parallel, they form a live, real-money systematic-vs-discretionary comparison — the honest "human alpha" test, properly separated into two real pools.

Guardrails for Strategy S (AI trading real money)

  • Hard per-trade risk cap and max position size; max open positions (e.g. 3–5).
  • Daily and total loss kill-switch — halts new entries past −X%, full stop past −Y%.
  • Stop-loss always resting at the broker (GTT), never software-only.
  • Idempotent order handling + broker reconciliation (no double-fire, no drift).
  • Shadow / micro-size proving period before any unsupervised autonomy; human-approval gate on entries in phase 1.
  • Risk-freeze and earnings-blackout checks block live entries automatically.

11 · Honest Risks & Open Questions

  • Overfitting — the perennial enemy; mitigated by walk-forward + deflation + stability checks, never eliminated.
  • Gap / tail risk — stops don't cover gaps (§1); residual tail remains.
  • Regime death — breakout edges bleed in prolonged chop/bear; the regime governor reduces but doesn't remove this.
  • Edge decay & capacity — published edges erode; small personal capital helps, but monitoring is required.
  • Execution gap — backtest fills vs. live fills, especially in NSE small/midcaps.
  • AI-autonomy risk — bugs/edge-cases in an autonomous money loop; mitigated by deterministic guardrails + kill-switch + shadow-first.
  • Multi-market transfer — the India edge may differ in the US; re-validate per market, don't assume.

12 · Go-Live Gates (must pass, in order)

  1. Honest backtest shows after-cost Calmar that beats both baselines, out-of-sample, with stable parameters.
  2. Monte-Carlo ruin probability negligible at chosen sizing.
  3. Paper / shadow period reproduces backtest behavior within tolerance.
  4. Guardrails (caps, kill-switch, resting stops, reconciliation) tested and proven.
  5. Then — and only then — micro-size live, scaling up as live tracks expectation.
