1 · Philosophy & Objective

A trend-aligned, volatility-contraction breakout system with volatility-normalized risk, expressed entirely in R-multiples. Lineage: Weinstein (stage analysis), Minervini (VCP contraction), O'Neil/Wyckoff (pivotal-candle accumulation), Van Tharp (R-multiple risk). It is a positive-skew system — many small fast losses, a few large winners — not a high-win-rate system.

The two design values

Maximize

The edge, measured as SQN = (mean R ÷ std R) × √N — risk- and sample-adjusted return in R. This is Van Tharp's own metric and is mathematically ≈ a deflated Sharpe of the trade R-distribution.

Predictability

An edge that is consistent out-of-sample — stable across walk-forward folds and regimes. We prefer a high and steady SQN over a lucky in-sample peak. Operationalized in §10.

Objective (locked): maximize SQN and predictability, subject to a hard maximum-drawdown ceiling. Unit = R-multiples. Reported diagnostics: expectancy (mean R), total R, win rate, ARR, Calmar, max DD, full R-distribution.

Why SQN, not raw R or raw Calmar

Raw R-expectancy ignores variance (drawdown) and is dominated by rare tails — it overfits. Raw Calmar is a single-path extremum, statistically unstable as a maximand. SQN keeps Afzal's R-multiple framework intact while making the optimizer immune to variance and small-sample luck. Calmar + the drawdown ceiling become constraints, which is strictly more protective of capital.

Win-rate vs R: expect ~35–45% win rate with 3–6R average winners. Pushing win rate (early scale-outs) shrinks average R and usually lowers expectancy. We optimize the edge, not the hit-rate.

Capital protection — and its honest limit

Stops + fixed-fractional risk bound intraday loss. They do not cover the overnight/earnings gap that opens below the stop — the dominant tail in swing trading. We defend it with gap-aware sizing, the earnings blackout (§6), overnight/cluster caps (§7), a kill-switch, and a backtest that fills stops at the worse of stop-or-next-open.

5 · Signal Definitions parameterized · learned · per-stock-relative

Single source of truth

All signals are computed in deterministic Python fed by Kite data. The legacy Pine scripts become optional charting aids only — they no longer drive scans, alerts, or trades, and must not diverge from the engine.

Base candle math: TRP = (High−Low)/Close×100; close_position = (Close−Low)/(High−Low); volume & TRP ratios vs the stock's own trailing window.

PPC — Positive Pivotal Candle

The legacy definition was four ANDed conditions with hard-coded thresholds (TRP-ratio ≥ 1.5×, close ≥ 0.60, volume ≥ 1.5×, green). v2 keeps the structure but changes two things that matter:

  • Thresholds are learned, not hard-coded — set by the Baseline Calibration (§10), re-validated over time.
  • Signals are relative to the stock's own baseline — TRP expansion vs its own history; delivery-% as a z-score vs the stock's own 50-day delivery distribution (not a flat ">60"); volume vs its own norm. "Pivotal" adapts to a sleepy large-cap vs. a volatile mid-cap instead of one universal rule.
  • Delivery-weighted volume on the pivot bar is the participation test — real shares changing hands, the NSE-specific institutional tell the US lacks.

NPC — Negative Pivotal Candle

Bearish mirror. Primary use: sector-weakness detection (avoid strong stocks in distributing sectors); short candidates in the US phase. Not forced into the long watchlist.

Contraction (the coil)

Real volatility compression: ATR declining (regression slope), candle ranges progressively narrowing (strict — fixes the legacy inverted tolerance), coiling near resistance. The trigger bar's high becomes the entry trigger.

Stage (Weinstein trend filter)

Trade only Stage 2 / confirmed Stage-1 breakout. Mutually-exclusive bands (no overlap → no topping-stock-as-buy), with the higher-highs + volume-on-breakout gates restored.

Candle is the trigger; context is the edge

A raw PPC alone is weak. It becomes a tradeable candidate only with context: base structure (depth, contraction count, base stage/count, prior advance — the Minervini/O'Neil core that was missing), a relative-strength gate (RS is a hard pre-filter, not a soft feature — never buy laggard breakouts), computed sector strength (PPC-vs-NPC breadth + industry-group leadership), and an earnings-clear window (§6).

6 · Entry · Stop · Exit · Sizing

Entry

Confirmed break of the trigger on the daily close (the EOD discipline avoids intraday head-fakes and matches the bar the signals are computed on). A follow-through / breakout-volume requirement filters weak breaks. The legacy "always 50/50 split" is dropped to a hypothesis — a single confirmed entry is tested against it (likely wins after costs).

Stop

Initial stop = entry − TRP value, volatility-normalized per stock, never widened, trailed up only. A tight fixed-% overlay near entry — stop = min(vol-stop, ~8% from entry) — enforces "small fast loss" discipline on high-ATR names.

Exit — ride winners

Council-mandated change: collapse the scale-out ladder

The legacy 2R/NE/GE/EE ladder fought the positive-skew thesis (it sold most of the position before the 20R move that makes the year) and was cost-suicidal (4–5 fee-bearing legs). v2 default: ride a single position to a structural trailing exit (21/50-EMA or base-support violation), with a climax-detection exit (acceleration + exhaustion gap + largest-volume bar) to bank parabolic runs. Minimal early de-risk only. Pyramiding into confirmed winners is tested as the mirror of scaling out. Exit-leg count is a calibrated parameter with the real cost model attached.

Sizing capital-agnostic

Fixed-fractional risk-per-trade (regime-scaled), expressed as % of capital so it works at any pool size. Optional fractional-Kelly cap (validated). The actual capital figure is set later and does not drive the design; the cost model (§7) is applied at whatever ticket size results.

Earnings / announcement handling Afzal rule + gap defense

  • No new entry if a scheduled result/major announcement falls within the next ~10 trading days (learnable window; Afzal's 1–2 weeks).
  • Asymmetric exception: a clean post-results gap that starts a fresh base is a valid setup — welcome it once uncertainty resolves.
  • Holdings: as a result approaches, reduce/exit based on profit cushion vs. stop distance.
  • This is the #1 gap defense — earnings are the largest source of overnight gap-throughs. Requires a lightweight earnings/corporate-actions calendar connector.

7 · Risk & Capital Protection

  • Per-trade: RPT (regime-scaled), volatility-normalized stop, fixed-% overlay.
  • Portfolio: a gap-stressed, correlation-aware open-risk cap (apply a learned adverse gap to all correlated positions simultaneously — not the naive sum of stop distances). Hard single-name and sector/cluster caps (the only defense against a limit-down/circuit on one name).
  • Drawdown governor: explicit equity-peak definition + cadence, a graduated de-risk ladder, and a written unfreeze rule (human sign-off and an objective regime-recovery condition — not either alone).
  • Ruin test: block-bootstrap that preserves correlation and resamples real overnight-gap/circuit sequences; a numeric ruin threshold and a numeric pass bar.
  • Circuits/halts: price-band screen at selection; consecutive-circuit sequences modeled in the ruin test.

8 · The Learning System — two loops

Keeping the loops separate is what makes this learning, not curve-fitting — but only if Loop 2 cannot fit the test set (below).

Loop 1 — Parameter learning (offline, validated)

Every threshold (§5–6) is a knob. For each candidate set, the honest backtest (real costs incl. the per-scrip DP charge + STT asymmetry, an ADT-keyed slippage distribution, worse-of-stop-or-gap fills) reports the R-distribution. A Bayesian search maximizes the §1 objective. Discipline:

  • Combinatorial Purged CV with purge + embargo sized to the holding period (no overlap leakage).
  • Report PBO (probability of backtest overfitting); gate < ~5%.
  • Power analysis per parameter and per regime cell; underpowered cells are not promoted.
  • Deflate by the true effective trial count (logged in a monotonic experiment ledger).
  • Placebo test: run the whole pipeline on synthetic/shuffled price series — it must find no edge.
  • Winners → dated, versioned configs, promoted to live only with human sign-off. Re-run quarterly, not nightly.

Loop 2 — Attribution (continuous)

Every signal/trade is tagged with features (stage, base quality, sector strength, delivery-% anomaly, contraction tightness, regime) and realized R, building a per-(signal×regime×sector) evidence table. It biases live ranking via Bayesian shrinkage (strong prior toward backtest expectancy; small weight at low N) and acts as a drift detector.

Loop 2 is HALT-ONLY

The drift detector may throttle, de-risk, or flatten — a safety action. It may never seed or bias the next optimization with live-P&L-conditioned priors; that would be fitting the test set one quarter at a time. Re-optimization always runs on the frozen historical lab with the pre-registered protocol.

Reality check (from the quant seat): at ~5–20 live trades/month, detecting a 50–60% edge decay takes years of live data — so the live loop is a safety net, not the primary learner. The edge is learned from a decade of history; live results confirm and de-risk.

9 · Regime Contextualization "how markets are doing today"

A measured regime, not a vibe: index trend (price vs rising 150-DMA), breadth (% of universe in Stage 2), volatility (India VIX, ATR percentile), factor leadership, distribution-day count (O'Neil's institutional-selling tell), and — critically — a tactical breakout-failure-rate monitor on the system's own recent triggers (the best anti-chop defense: when breakouts are failing fast, throttle regardless of what the slow 150-DMA says). The regime scales three dials: RPT, max positions, selectivity; in weak regimes the system de-risks automatically or goes flat.

10 · Baseline Calibration — getting to the right levels for today ★

This is how we set "today's levels" — the actual PPC/NPC thresholds, contraction/stage bands, exit parameters, regime dials — from historical data, not by hand.

  1. Run the honest backtest over 10 years of Kite history for each candidate parameter set, producing its R-distribution across K walk-forward folds and across regimes.
  2. Score for maximization AND predictability. We do not pick the highest in-sample config. We maximize a mean-variance objective on the edge itself:
    score = mean(OOS SQN) − λ · std(OOS SQN)   subject to   maxDD ≤ ceiling and PBO < 5%
    High mean OOS SQN = the edge (maximization). Low dispersion of OOS SQN across folds/regimes = the edge is consistent and repeatable (predictability). A lucky in-sample peak with wild fold-to-fold swings is rejected even if its average looks great.
  3. Emit a dated "Baseline Card" — the chosen levels + their expected behavior (expectancy, SQN, Calmar, max DD, each with confidence intervals) + the regimes where they work and where they don't.
  4. Forward-confirm by simulation — paper/shadow + Monte-Carlo before the levels go live.
  5. Re-calibrate quarterly (or on a drift trigger) so the baseline tracks how the market is behaving now, while staying validated.

What runs "as of today" during development

The first thing we build and execute is this calibration on 10y of Kite data. Its output is the system's live baseline — the levels that define PPC/NPC and everything else right now — chosen because they were both profitable and predictable out-of-sample, not because someone typed "1.5×".

11 · Operating Model — two strategies paper-first

Strategy S — Systematic

Claude-operated on the rails; trades only calibrated, council-vetted signals; all guardrails enforced. The machine.

Strategy D — Discretionary

You take the calls; the system supplies vetted setup cards, sizing, risk, and the journal. The human.

Run in parallel = a live, honest systematic-vs-discretionary comparison. Capital is a free variable set later; both start as paper/shadow and go live only after the gates pass, at a viable size. AI guardrails for Strategy S: heartbeat/dead-man's switch (escalate on broker/token loss while exposed), post-fill risk reconciliation (re-derive risk from filled qty), rate-limiting distinct from idempotency, max-notional ceiling, and a kill-switch defined as flatten-or-freeze with a stated latency.

"No-go / return capital" is an acceptable outcome if the edge doesn't survive honest costs.

14 · Honest Risks & Return Expectations

Risks: overfitting (mitigated, never eliminated); gap/tail risk (stops don't cover gaps); regime death in prolonged chop; edge decay; execution gap vs backtest; AI-autonomy bugs; multi-market non-transfer (re-validate, never assume).

Return expectation — no quota

We target the process (max SQN + predictability under a drawdown cap), not a monthly number. Returns will be lumpy and regime-dependent: strong in trending years, flat/negative in chop. 2–3%/month (~27–43% annualized) is a good-regime aspiration, not a sustained monthly guarantee — and chasing it as a quota requires more risk, which fights capital protection. The credible figure comes from the Baseline Calibration backtest; any system that promises 2–3%/month is lying.
