5 · Drawdown Decomposition — Why -24% with a 10% Stop?

Important distinction: the 10% stop caps the loss on each position, measured from its entry price. Portfolio drawdown measures give-back from the equity peak — a completely different quantity. The maximum a stop-loss can bound is 15×0.5% = 7.5% (all positions fresh and stopped same day). The -24% is not capital loss — it is accumulated paper profit being given back on multi-bagger winners whose stops sat 50–90% below their current price.

8 · Trade Return Distribution

The shape that makes the strategy work: many small outcomes, a long fat right tail of multi-baggers. Numbers under each bar = total P&L from that bucket.
Asymmetry is the engine. Losses are tightly clustered (the 10% stop truncates the left tail), while winners extend to +500% and beyond. The top 10 trades alone account for 71% of all gross profit; the top 20 for 85%. This is concentration risk to be aware of — but it is the expected signature of trend-following, not a flaw.

16 · Methodology & Assumptions

Entry signalRS ratio (Stock Close ÷ Nifty 50 Close) EMA50 crosses above EMA200
Exit signalRS EMA50 crosses below EMA200 (symmetric), OR price hits 10% hard stop from entry
ExecutionSignal at close; fill at next day's open; unfilled orders expire after 1 day
Position sizingFixed risk: Capital × 0.5% ÷ 10% = ₹50,000 notional per trade
Universe filterMean daily turnover ≥ ₹5 crore across the sim period (429 stocks qualify)
CapacityMax 15 concurrent positions; new signals queue and fill as slots free up
Data sourceDaily OHLCV, auto-adjusted for splits/dividends
Not modelledBrokerage, STT, slippage, impact cost. Returns are gross of transaction costs (tax IS modelled in §14)
Caveats for the desk: (1) Backtest assumes next-open fills with no slippage. (2) The 24% drawdown is real MTM give-back and must be tolerable to capture the 24% CAGR. (3) Survivorship: universe is current NSE liquid names; delisted/merged stocks are excluded. (4) Returns are concentrated in a few multi-baggers (top 10 trades = 71% of gross profit). (5) 2021–2025 was broadly bullish for Indian equities — forward returns are regime-dependent.
