Operating Synopsis · v2 strategy

How the Champion Trader System Will Work

What we're building, how it runs each day, and the life of a single trade — in plain language, before any code is written.

01In one paragraph

A fully-automated swing-trading system that runs the validated v2 strategy on liquid NSE stocks (≥ ₹5cr/day turnover) through Zerodha / Kite. After each market close it scans for breakout setups and builds tomorrow's watchlist. In the last 30 minutes of the next session it enters names that break their trigger on confirming volume. It then manages every position with a close-based 5×ATR trailing stop — riding winners, cutting losers only on a confirmed close below the stop — with sizing and risk on fixed rules (0.35% risk/trade · max 15 positions · quarter-size in bear markets · halt at 15% drawdown). You supervise from a dashboard and Telegram, in auto or approve-each-action mode. Phase 1 is paper (no real money); Phase 2 places live orders.

02One brain, many hands

The validated v2 logic lives in one place — backend/engine/runtime/. Everything else (scanner, monitors, autopilot, alerts) calls this brain. There is no second strategy. Already proven: it reproduces the backtest trade-for-trade, 293 / 293.

signal_service
"Is this a v2 entry today?"
→ a trigger price + stop distance, or nothing.
exit_service
"What do I do with this open position?"
→ hold & ratchet the 5×ATR trail, or exit.
risk_manager
"How many shares — and am I allowed?"
→ size, after caps / bear-sizing / drawdown checks.

03A day in the life of the system (IST)

The system's real moment is the last 30 minutes — when both entries and exits execute, the way a swing trader runs it ("review at EOD, act near the close"). Everything else is prep.

09:15
Morning gap-check. Any open position that gaps open below its trailing stop is exited at the open (overnight gap risk).
09:15–15:00
Idle for decisions — v2 acts on the close, not intraday wiggles. (optional non-binding watch only)
15:00–15:30
THE ACTION WINDOWentries + exits
① Entries: each READY watchlist name trading above its trigger on ≥2× volume → sized → BUY near the close.
② Exits: any open position trading below its trail → SELL near the close (a confirmed close-below).
15:30
Market close.
~15:45
Data ingest. Pull the day's Kite-adjusted bars for the universe + NIFTY 500 into the database.
16:00
Trail update + scan. Ratchet every open trail up (stop = max(old, high − 5×ATR)); run the scan → write today's setups → build tomorrow's READY/NEAR watchlist.
16:45
Regime check. Is NIFTY 500 above its rising 50-DMA? Sets tomorrow's sizing — full vs 0.25× (bear).
17:00
Daily brief → Telegram: new setups, watchlist, open positions + trails, today's exits, equity / open risk / drawdown.
overnight
Nothing. AutoOptimize is frozen — thresholds don't auto-change.
Why this is simpler than the old system: because v2 is close-based, it does not watch positions tick-by-tick (the old 2-min polling that caused premature exits). One decision near the close + one morning gap-check. Less infrastructure — and it matches the method.

04The life of a single trade

Spotted  — Day D, after close
Stock X passes the v2 scan (Stage-2 uptrend, volatility contraction, valid base, avg TRP ≥ 2) → lands on the watchlist as READY, with a trigger price T (the 5-day high) and a stop distance.
Entered  — a later day, last 30 min
X trades above T on ≥2× average volume → size = 0.35% of equity ÷ stop-distance, ×0.25 if bear regime, skipped if already 15 positions / drawdown halt / circuit-locked → BUY near close. Initial stop = entry − 1×TRP.
Managed  — every day after, at the close
The trail ratchets up: stop = max(old, highest_high − 5×ATR). No profit target — the position rides.
Exited
The first day X closes below the trail (or gaps below at the open) → SELL the whole position. R-multiple & P&L recorded, Telegram notifies, journal updates.
The honest trade reality: ~65% of trades exit for a small loss (~−1R); the minority that trend pay for them many times over. You must take every signal — the winners are unpredictable and a handful make the year.

05The safeguards (always on)

Per-trade risk 0.35% of equity, and a hard max of 15 concurrent positions.
Bear-sizing: quarter-size new entries when NIFTY 500 is below its rising 50-DMA.
Drawdown breaker: at −15% from peak, halt new entries; resume within 7.5%. Open trades keep running.
Liquidity floor ≥ ₹5cr/day turnover (config; ≥ ₹15cr for large capital) — fillable, less survivorship-prone.
Kill-switch (Phase 2): one control to stop all new orders instantly.
Idle cash parked at ~6.5% so un-deployed capital still earns.

07Data & broker — one provider

Zerodha / Kite supplies both the adjusted historical bars the brain reasons on and the order execution. One login, one session — and the live feed is identical to the backtest feed, which removes the price-adjustment mismatch that caused the original phantom-loss bug. The old yfinance scan feed and the Dhan stubs are retired.

08The honest expectations

Plan around ~16–18% deployable CAGR (ex-2021), not the 26.5% headline. That headline leaned on sub-₹1cr micro-caps you can't fill at size and on the one-off 2021 melt-up. Win rate ~35%, win/loss magnitude ~8:1, with long stretches of small losses between trends. Flat years happen (2018, 2022 ≈ 0). Drawdowns to ~15–17% are normal; the breaker caps the tail.

09What gets built, in order

Phase 0 — Foundationin progress

Done: signal_service + exit_service extracted & parity-proven (293/293). Next: risk_manager + portfolio parity · trailing-stop DB column · Kite adjusted-bar ingest.

Phase 1 — Paper on the validated enginenext

Wire scan → watchlist → last-30-min entry → sizing → close-based exit → trades/journal → Telegram, all calling the runtime. Run in shadow beside the old path, then paper for 10–15 sessions; reconcile real fills. No real money.

Phase 2 — Live on Kitelater

Real orders behind the same runtime, ≥₹15cr tier if capital is large, hard risk gates + kill-switch + daily broker reconciliation.

The validated strategy is fixed and proven; this system is the disciplined machine that executes it without emotion — takes every signal, rides winners, and never widens a stop.
